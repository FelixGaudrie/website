This is Reachy. He’s pretty unique.

Reachy is an expressive open source humanoid robot. It’’s particularly good at interacting with people and manipulating objects.

Reachy uses prepackaged AI, image and voice recognition and modular robotics to interact with the world around him. No need to train up the machine learning component: jump straight into any desired application and start prototyping.

Now that’s a cute head

Reachy has a free-moving head animated by in-house developed Orbita© technology and antennas to convey emotions to its audience (happy, sad, excited...). It mimics human expressions and body language in its own unique way: we guarantee Reachy will make you smile!

And some capable robotic arms

With 7 DOF, Reachy’s arms feature similar dimensions, proportions and movement as in an adult human arm. One arm can lift objects that weight up to 500 grams and manipulate them with pretty good dexterity.

Open source platform

Reachy comes with ready-to-use operational environments (like playing tic tac toe for example) but because it can be easily programmed in Python, you’ll be able to build any new applications for your robot.

Reachy is super versatile.

Whether you’re a researcher, academic, creative or an innovation professional, Reachy is a pretty cool solution for you to explore and develop AI and robotics.

Check out some of the existing applications:

Academics

Howest is a Belgian technical university. Their ICT Bachelor students use Reachy as a tangible and visual solution to explore AI.

Healthcare

Medforall is an American company that provides high quality patient care in the State of Ohio and aims to make Reachy a companion dependent home-patients (presenting a physical handicap or cognitive disorders).

Retail

Mercari is a Japanese e-commerce company that provides a selling app and marketplace. They use Reachy in their delivery service where the robot can pick up packages and hand them out to customers.

Try it yourself. Right now!

Our open source tool will let you send commands using our Python’s API to a 3D model of a full Reachy robot (both arms and a head) and see their effect on the simulated model.
